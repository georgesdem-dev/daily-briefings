🚢 Hormuz Standoff: Mutual Blockades Deepen Economic Damage

The Strait of Hormuz enters its third week of effective closure. The US Navy maintains a blockade preventing Iranian oil exports; Tehran restricts or tolls commercial transits through the strait. Global energy analysts note that the prolonged disruption is reshaping tanker routes — some very large crude carriers (VLCCs) have begun rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope, adding 10–15 days to delivery times and significantly increasing shipping costs for Asian importers.

Critical CISA Adds 6 Known-Exploited Flaws: Fortinet, Microsoft, Adobe

CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog expanded this weekend with six new entries covering Fortinet FortiClient EMS (CVE-2026-21643, SQL injection, CVSS 9.1), Microsoft Exchange, and Adobe Acrobat. Federal Civilian Executive Branch agencies were given an April 27 deadline to apply mitigations. The Fortinet flaw allows unauthenticated attackers to execute code via crafted HTTP requests — a critical perimeter risk for any enterprise with FortiClient EMS exposed to the internet.

Sources: The Hacker News · CISA KEV Catalog

High Supply Chain Campaign Targets Security and Dev Tools

The Register and Semgrep researchers disclosed an ongoing supply-chain attack campaign specifically targeting security and developer tooling packages. Threat actors are compromising package maintainer accounts — particularly those lacking MFA — and pushing malicious versions that execute stealthily on import. The campaign predates the PyTorch Lightning incident later in the week and suggests a coordinated, sustained effort to compromise developer infrastructure across multiple ecosystems including npm, PyPI, and RubyGems.

Sources: The Register

High Itron Discloses April 13 Breach of Critical Infrastructure Systems

Itron — a major supplier of smart meters, energy management, and water monitoring systems powering utilities across North America — disclosed it detected unauthorized access to internal IT systems on April 13. The company said operations continued in all material respects and no customer-hosted systems were compromised. No ransomware group has claimed the attack. The disclosure is notable given Itron's footprint in critical infrastructure: its systems manage billing and operational data for hundreds of utility providers.
