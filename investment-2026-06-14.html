🕊️ Iran War Day 107: The Islamabad MOU — A Peace Deal Is Within Reach

The single most important macro story for markets entering Q3 2026: US and Iranian negotiators reached agreement on the final text of the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding on June 12, addressing active hostilities, oil sanctions relief, and partial Strait of Hormuz reopening. President Trump has signalled an imminent formal signing ceremony — possibly in Europe within days — which would end a conflict that has been the dominant geopolitical force of 2026.

Markets are pricing in a high probability of success: WTI crude dropped -3.23% to $84.88/bbl on Friday — down sharply from $102/bbl in early May. The Strait of Hormuz, effectively closed since January, would reopen progressively, potentially adding 15–20% to global oil supply within 60 days. Key risk: Iran's Supreme Leader has not yet provided final authorisation, and significant interpretive gaps remain between the two sides on nuclear issues.

Investment implication: A formal ceasefire collapses the remaining geopolitical risk premium in oil markets (XOM, CVX headwinds) while unleashing a risk-on rotation into Financials (JPM, BAC) and Consumer Cyclicals. Defense stocks (RTX, LMT) could see profit-taking. Airlines and global shippers are the most direct beneficiaries of Hormuz reopening — watch XAL (airline ETF) and ZIM.

🥇 The Gold Paradox: Up +3% in a Risk-On Day — What It Means

Friday's most intriguing signal: gold surged +3.03% to $4,238/oz on a day when the VIX collapsed -9% and equities rallied. Classic risk-on environments are bearish for gold. The divergence tells a crucial story: institutional investors are simultaneously buying equities AND gold — a portfolio allocation that signals deep concern about the US fiscal trajectory.

Ray Dalio has called the skyrocketing national debt a "heart attack" threatening the US economy. With the 10-year Treasury yield rising +2.4bps to 4.487%, bond markets are not celebrating the peace deal — they're demanding more compensation for inflation risk and fiscal uncertainty. Gold at $4,239 (down from the $4,575 high of May, but rebounding) is institutional money hedging against the scenario where: (1) oil normalizes but core inflation stays sticky; (2) the Fed keeps rates at 4.5%+ through 2026; (3) a hard landing becomes the base case in Q4 2026.

Portfolio implication: The gold signal supports Howard Marks' framework — maintain 10–15% in non-correlated alternatives. Consider GLD or physical gold as a portfolio hedge alongside the equity picks below.

Ray Dalio / Bridgewater Associates

National Debt Is a "Heart Attack" — The Silent Risk Hiding Behind the AI Bull Run

Ray Dalio's most urgent warning for 2026 centres on the US fiscal trajectory: the national debt is growing at a pace he describes as a "heart attack" threatening long-term economic stability. While equity markets are celebrating AI earnings and a potential Iran peace deal, the bond market is quietly raising its inflation and default-risk premium — the 10-year Treasury yield at 4.487% is the canary in the coal mine. Dalio's prescription: gold (currently validating this thesis at $4,239, up +3% even in a risk-on session), diversification across global assets, and avoidance of over-concentration in US dollar-denominated assets. The divergence between equities (optimistic) and gold/bonds (cautious) is the defining tension of H2 2026 investing.

Howard Marks / Oaktree Capital

"Selectivity Over Breadth" — Not the Time for Maximum Risk

Howard Marks' framework for 2026: with the S&P at 7,431 and VIX at 17.68 (near multi-year lows), investors are pricing in a benign scenario that requires multiple things to go right simultaneously — Iran peace deal finalised, Fed cuts materialise, AI earnings continue beating, no hard landing. Marks' prescription for sophisticated investors: increase allocation to income-generating quality (dividend growers, corporate bonds), reduce or avoid names at 100x+ earnings multiples, and maintain 10–15% in non-correlated alternatives (gold, real assets) for optionality. "We are not in a safe market" is his overriding message. This brief's moderate-risk picks — MSFT at 23x earnings, JNJ at ~26x, JPM at 14.8x, BRK-B at ~14x — are designed to reflect Marks' framework rather than momentum chasing.
