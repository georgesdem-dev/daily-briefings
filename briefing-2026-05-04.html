Breaking US Navy Begins Hormuz Escort Operations; Iran Calls It a Ceasefire Violation

The Trump administration launched its plan to escort American-flagged commercial ships through the Strait of Hormuz on Monday. Two vessels successfully transited the strait under naval protection — the first such passage since Iran imposed shipping restrictions. Iran's IRGC responded sharply, with a senior parliament security official warning that US military escorts violate the terms of the ceasefire brokered by Pakistan on April 8. Iran had conditionally allowed commercial traffic during the ceasefire but re-imposed restrictions when Washington refused to lift its naval blockade of Iranian ports.

Diplomatic Iran's 14-Point Proposal Hits Wall Over Nuclear Demand

Iran presented Washington — via Pakistani intermediaries — with a 14-point peace framework calling for: guaranteed non-aggression, comprehensive sanctions relief, lifting of the naval blockade, resolution of the Lebanon front, and a protocol to fully reopen Hormuz. Crucially, the plan defers nuclear negotiations to a later phase, after economic relief is secured. The Trump administration has rejected this sequencing. Secretary of State Marco Rubio reiterated on Sunday that any final deal must include Iran's permanent dismantlement of its nuclear program — a condition Tehran calls a "red line" of its own. Negotiations remain at an impasse.

Critical ShinyHunters Claims 275M-Record Breach of Instructure / Canvas LMS

The ShinyHunters extortion group added edtech giant Instructure — maker of Canvas LMS — to its Tor-based leak site on May 3, claiming the theft of 3.65 terabytes of data. The group asserts records belonging to approximately 275 million students, teachers, and staff across nearly 9,000 educational institutions worldwide were compromised. Exfiltrated data reportedly includes names, email addresses, and student ID numbers. Instructure disclosed a breach and scrambled to restore services over the weekend. With Canvas holding a dominant share of the US higher-education LMS market, the blast radius spans most major US universities and K-12 systems.

CISA KEV Windows Zero-Click CVE-2026-32202: Federal Patch Deadline May 12

Microsoft and CISA confirmed the flaw is being actively exploited in the wild. APT28 (Russia's GRU unit) was previously linked to exploitation of this vulnerability class. IAB Zestix/Sentap IAB Linked to Dozens of Enterprise Breaches via Infostealers

SecurityWeek reports that a threat actor identified as Zestix/Sentap — functioning as an initial access broker — has been tied to data breaches at dozens of major global enterprises. The actor uses credentials harvested by infostealer malware to gain entry, then sells access or facilitates follow-on attacks. The campaign underscores the persistent threat posed by the infostealer-to-IAB pipeline, where a single piece of commodity malware infecting an employee device can cascade into a full corporate breach.
