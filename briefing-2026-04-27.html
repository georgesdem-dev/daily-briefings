📜 Iran's Formal Proposal: Open Hormuz, Lift Blockade, Defer Nuclear Talks

Iran formally submitted its peace proposal to the United States via Pakistani mediators: reopen the Strait of Hormuz in exchange for the US lifting its naval blockade and formally ending the war — with Iran's nuclear programme addressed only in a subsequent, separate negotiation phase. The proposal envisions an extended ceasefire or permanent end to hostilities as the first stage, Hormuz reopening as the second, and nuclear talks as a later third phase.

Secretary of State Marco Rubio publicly called the proposal "unacceptable," stating that the US will not agree to lift its blockade without guarantees on Iran's nuclear programme. Trump's White House said the president was "reviewing" the proposal with national security advisors.

🔒 Cybersecurity THREAT INTEL

Critical CVE-2026-32202 Active Exploitation Confirmed — APT28 Incomplete Patch Creates New Zero-Click Attack

Microsoft confirmed on April 27 that CVE-2026-32202 is under active exploitation. The flaw stems from an incomplete patch for CVE-2026-21510 — a vulnerability previously exploited by Russia's APT28 (Fancy Bear) via weaponized .lnk shortcut files. Microsoft patched CVE-2026-21510 in April's Patch Tuesday but marked it incorrectly; the fix was insufficient, and attackers quickly weaponised the residual gap. Browsing a folder containing a malicious shortcut is sufficient to send the victim's NTLMv2 hash to an attacker — enabling relay attacks and password recovery. CISA's mandatory patch deadline for federal agencies is May 12.

Sources: The Hacker News · Akamai · Help Net Security

High Itron Breach Details: Utility Smart Meter Giant Hit in April 13 Intrusion

Itron — which provides smart metering and energy management infrastructure to hundreds of North American utilities — disclosed full details of its April 13 intrusion. The attacker gained access to internal IT systems; Itron confirmed no customer-hosted systems were reached and operations were unaffected. However, the attack's origin and whether data was exfiltrated remain unknown. No ransomware group has claimed responsibility. The incident underscores persistent threat actor interest in OT-adjacent IT systems that carry operational data for water, power, and gas infrastructure.

Sources: TechCrunch · Bleeping Computer · SecurityWeek

High VECT 2.0 RaaS: Marketed as Ransomware, Operates as a Data Wiper

Researchers published analysis of VECT 2.0, a cross-platform Ransomware-as-a-Service operation targeting Windows, Linux, and VMware ESXi environments. Forensic analysis reveals a critical flaw in VECT 2.0's cryptographic engine: files larger than 128 KB are permanently and unrecoverably destroyed rather than encrypted, making VECT 2.0 function as a catastrophic wiper rather than a traditional extortion tool. Organisations targeted by VECT 2.0 face permanent data loss — rendering victim-side decryption keys meaningless.

Source: The Hacker News
