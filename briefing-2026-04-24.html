Critical Three Microsoft Defender Zero-Days Actively Exploited — Two Still Unpatched

Three Defender zero-days have been exploited since April 10, with attackers leveraging privilege escalation and denial-of-service paths. The most severe — CVE-2026-33825 "BlueHammer" — is a time-of-check/time-of-use flaw in Defender's signature-update mechanism that allows a low-privilege attacker to gain SYSTEM. Two of the three CVEs remain unpatched as of today; Microsoft says patches are in accelerated development.

Separately, the exploited SharePoint CVE-2026-32201 (spoofing, CVSS 6.5) was addressed in this month's Patch Tuesday alongside 160 other vulnerabilities.

Sources: The Hacker News · SecurityWeek

Critical Storm-1175: Medusa Ransomware Deployed Within 24 Hours

Microsoft Threat Intelligence has published an updated profile of Storm-1175, a China-linked group that has radically compressed the ransomware attack lifecycle. Average dwell time across the industry is 21 days; Storm-1175 moves from initial exploitation to Medusa ransomware delivery in under 24 hours. The group weaponises zero-days approximately one week before public disclosure — recent examples include CVE-2025-10035 (Fortra GoAnywhere MFT) and CVE-2026-23760 (SmarterTools SmarterMail). Sectors hit hardest: healthcare, education, financial services, professional services — primarily in the US, UK, and Australia.

Sources: The Hacker News · Microsoft Security Blog · Dark Reading
