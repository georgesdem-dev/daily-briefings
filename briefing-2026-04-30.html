🗳️ Senate Rejects War Powers Resolution 50-47 — Collins Crosses Party Lines

The US Senate voted 50-47 to reject a Democratic War Powers Resolution that would have forced removal of US forces from Iran — a narrow defeat that marks the sixth failed attempt since hostilities began. The vote came just one day before the contested 60-day legal deadline under the War Powers Act of 1973. In a historic break, Republican Sen. Susan Collins of Maine voted with Democrats for the first time, calling the Act's deadline "not a suggestion — it is a requirement." Sen. Rand Paul also voted in favour. The Senate then adjourned for a week-long recess.
